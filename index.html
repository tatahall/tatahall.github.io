About Me

Adapt~Improvise~Overcome

I want to be a doctor when I grow up. That’s what I told everyone around me from the sixth grade until graduate school. Two years of graduate school later and I finally admitted the truth – I didn’t want to be a doctor anymore. The problem was that I didn’t know what I wanted to be or where I wanted to go.

My overarching goal was this: no matter where I went or what I did, I wanted to be challenged; I wanted to be constantly learning new skills; I wanted to be in a position of being able to share what I had learned; I wanted to help people – even if it was a tangential effect.
